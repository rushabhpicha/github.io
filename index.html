Cyber Bullying Detection

Automatic cyberbullying detection is a task of growing interest, particularly in the Natural Language Processing and Machine Learning communities. Not only is it challenging, but it is also a relevant need given how social networks have become a vital part of individuals' lives and how dire the consequences of cyberbullying can be, especially among adolescents. We employed it on Tweets

Successful prevention depends on the adequate detection of potentially harmful messages and the information overload on the Web requires intelligent systems to identify potential risks automatically. Therefore, we employed Naive Bayes algorithm to improve the accuracy from 57% to 86% based on the profanity score assigned. It could also detect sarastic comments posted on Twitter.

Optimization of Taxi Revenue

Taxis are an essential component of the transportation system in most urban centers like New York. The ability to optimize the efficiency of routing represents an opportunity to increase revenues for taxi drivers. The vacant taxis cruising on the roads are not only wasting fuel consumption, the time of a taxi driver, and create unnecessary carbon emissions but also generate additional traffic in the city.Authored a technical paper which aims at improving the taxi revenue of the conventional taxicabs who are facing a huge loss due to car aggregators like Lyft and Uber. Q-learning technique was implemented to suggest optimal driving strategies to the drivers which would not only provide optimized route for the next trip, but for the entire span of day.

Personalized Medicine

Taking up the Kaggle challenge “Personalized Medicine: Redefining Cancer Treatment” propelled my interests towards the application of Data Science in Healthcare.‘One size does not fit all’ is the tenet of personalized medicine. Recently, personalized medicine is being applied to the entire spectrum of healthcare including cancer. For breast cancers, there are about 180 oncogenic mutations that contribute to the growth of a tumor. By identifying the profile of a patient’s genetic mutations, doctors can tailor personalized drug therapy in order to ensure better patient care. I used Word2Vec model for extracting vector representation of genes and mutations, which were expected to be informative about its effect. With Random Forest classifier, accuracy came up to 80% and all genetic mutations were classified in 9 different classes.
